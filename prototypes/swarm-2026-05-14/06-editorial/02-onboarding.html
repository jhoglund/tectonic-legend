For the newcomer

How to read a Tectonic.

A short guide. Reading time: about two minutes.

A Tectonic is a small grid divided into cages — clusters of two to five cells outlined in heavier ink. Each cage holds the numbers 1 through N, where N is its size. A pair holds 1 and 2. A trio holds 1, 2, 3. And so on.

There is a second rule, and it is the one that gives the puzzle its texture: cells that touch — including diagonally — cannot share a number. A cell sees its eight neighbours.

Where to look first

Begin with cages that have the fewest empty cells. Then look for cells whose neighbours have already used most of the available numbers. The puzzle reveals itself in layers.

Patience is the first technique.

We'll teach you the rest as you go — naked singles, hidden singles, forced moves, and eventually contradiction chains. You don't have to memorise the names. The names will arrive on their own.
